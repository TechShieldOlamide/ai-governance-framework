## Purpose

You cannot govern what you cannot see. This template helps organisations maintain a complete inventory of all AI systems in use, including third party tools, internal models, and AI-powered features embedded in existing software.

---

## Why an AI Inventory Matters

Most AI governance failures begin with visibility gaps. An engineering team deploys a new feature. A product manager enables an AI integration. A customer service team starts using a new tool. None of it is recorded centrally, and the compliance team finds out months later when something goes wrong.

An AI inventory is the foundation of any AI governance program. Before you can assess risk, implement controls, or demonstrate compliance, you need to know what AI systems exist in your organisation.

---

## How to Use This Template

1. Identify all AI systems in use across every department
2. Complete one row per AI system
3. Review and update the inventory quarterly
4. Flag any new AI deployments before they go live

## AI Deployment Approval Process

Before any new AI system is deployed, the following approvals must be obtained:

- [ ] Business justification documented
- [ ] Data inputs and outputs identified
- [ ] Personal data processing assessed
- [ ] EU AI Act risk level classified
- [ ] Risk assessment completed
- [ ] Privacy impact assessment conducted if personal data is processed
- [ ] Legal or compliance review completed
- [ ] System owner assigned
- [ ] Added to AI inventory

---

## Quarterly Review Checklist

- [ ] All active AI systems confirmed still in use
- [ ] Any new AI systems added to inventory
- [ ] Any retired AI systems marked as inactive
- [ ] Risk levels reviewed for accuracy
- [ ] System owners confirmed
- [ ] Third party vendor AI policies reviewed for changes
