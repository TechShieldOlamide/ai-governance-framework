## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for the responsible development, procurement, deployment, and monitoring of artificial intelligence systems within [Organisation Name]. It ensures that AI is used in a way that is transparent, accountable, fair, and compliant with applicable laws and regulations including the EU AI Act 2024.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who develop, procure, deploy, or use AI systems on behalf of [Organisation Name], including:

- Internally developed AI models and algorithms
- Third party AI tools and platforms
- AI-powered features embedded in existing software
- Automated decision-making systems

---

## 3. AI Governance Principles

**3.1 Human Oversight**
AI systems must support human decision-making, not replace it for consequential decisions. Appropriate human oversight must be maintained for all AI systems that affect individuals.

**3.2 Transparency**
Users must be informed when they are interacting with an AI system. AI decision-making processes must be explainable to the extent technically feasible.

**3.3 Fairness and Non-Discrimination**
AI systems must not produce discriminatory outcomes based on protected characteristics. Training data and model outputs must be reviewed for bias before deployment and monitored on an ongoing basis.

**3.4 Accountability**
Every AI system must have a designated system owner who is accountable for its performance, compliance, and impact. Responsibility for AI decisions cannot be delegated entirely to the machine.

**3.5 Data Protection**
AI systems that process personal data must comply with applicable data protection laws including GDPR and NDPA 2023. Privacy by design principles must be applied from the earliest stage of AI development or procurement.

**3.6 Security and Robustness**
AI systems must be designed and maintained to be resilient against adversarial attacks, data poisoning, and unexpected inputs. Security assessments must be conducted before deployment and after significant changes.

**3.7 Proportionality**
The use of AI must be proportionate to the business objective. AI should not be deployed where simpler, less risky approaches would achieve the same outcome.

---

## 4. AI Inventory

All AI systems in use across the organisation must be documented in the AI Inventory. No AI system may be deployed without being added to the inventory.

The AI Inventory must record:
- System name and purpose
- Vendor or internal development status
- Data inputs and outputs
- Personal data processed
- EU AI Act risk classification
- System owner
- Deployment date

The AI Inventory is reviewed and updated quarterly.

---

## 5. AI Risk Classification

All AI systems must be classified under the EU AI Act risk framework:

- Prohibited — must not be deployed
- High Risk — requires conformity assessment and ongoing monitoring
- Limited Risk — transparency obligations apply
- Minimal Risk — voluntary best practices recommended

Risk classification is conducted using the EU AI Act Risk Classification Checklist before any new AI system is deployed.

---

## 6. AI Procurement

Before procuring any third party AI tool or platform, the following must be completed:

- AI Vendor Due Diligence Checklist completed
- Data Processing Agreement reviewed and signed
- EU AI Act risk classification confirmed
- Privacy impact assessment conducted where personal data is processed
- Approval obtained from Compliance Lead and relevant system owner

---

## 7. AI Deployment

Before any AI system goes live, the following approvals are required:

- AI Risk Assessment completed and approved
- EU AI Act risk classification confirmed
- Technical documentation prepared for High Risk systems
- Human oversight mechanisms confirmed
- Staff training completed
- Incident response procedure confirmed
- System added to AI Inventory

---

## 8. Human Oversight Requirements

For AI systems that make or influence decisions affecting individuals:

- A human must be able to review, override, or correct any AI decision
- Affected individuals must be informed that AI was used in a decision
- Affected individuals must be able to request human review
- Oversight responsibilities must be clearly assigned and documented

---

## 9. Monitoring and Review

All deployed AI systems must be monitored on an ongoing basis for:

- Accuracy and performance degradation
- Bias and discriminatory outcomes
- Unexpected or harmful outputs
- Security vulnerabilities
- Changes in the regulatory environment

High Risk systems are reviewed quarterly. All other systems are reviewed annually.

---

## 10. AI Incident Response

When an AI system causes harm, produces a significant error, or behaves unexpectedly, the AI Incident Response Plan must be followed immediately.

All AI incidents must be reported to the Compliance Lead within 24 hours of discovery.

---

## 11. Prohibited AI Practices

The following AI practices are prohibited under this policy and the EU AI Act:

- Social scoring systems that evaluate individuals based on behaviour
- Real-time biometric identification in public spaces
- AI systems that exploit psychological vulnerabilities
- Emotion inference in workplace or educational settings
- Subliminal manipulation techniques

---

## 12. Staff Responsibilities

All staff who use AI tools must:

- Complete AI awareness training before using AI systems for business purposes
- Report any unexpected, harmful, or concerning AI behaviour immediately
- Not use AI tools that have not been approved through the procurement process
- Not input confidential or personal data into unapproved AI tools

---

## 13. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract, and may also result in regulatory action where applicable law has been breached.

---

## 14. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually or following any significant change to the organisation's AI use or the applicable regulatory environment.
